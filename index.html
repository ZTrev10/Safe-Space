SAFE SPACE

Find the wound. Build the scare.

The Safe Space Fellowship brings eight emerging horror writers of color to the White-Duvall Vineyard Estate on Catalina Island for five days of craft, mentorship, and industry access, helping them shape the work only they can write. One selected fellow will receive a first-look development deal.

About the Fellowship

Horror is craft. Fear is material. The fellowship is where both are put to work.

This exclusive fellowship is designed for motivated writers ready to sharpen their craft, interrogate fear, and uncover the story beneath the script.

Included in the Fellowship

  • Daily writing sessions
  • Cohort script analysis
  • Private 1:1 mentorship
  • Industry conversations
  • Final pitch consultation

The Estate

A private vineyard property selected for focus, privacy, and total immersion.

Fellows stay on site for the full program, sharing meals, pages, screenings, and late-night conversations across the White-Duvall family estate. The distance is part of the design.

Property subject to availability and may change at the host's discretion. Certain areas may be restricted.

The Opportunity

One selected fellow will receive a first-look development deal.

Participant Notes

  • Accepted fellows must be available for the full duration of the retreat.
  • Outside visitors are not permitted.
  • Cell service may be limited.
  • Participation requires a signed release.
  • Fellows are expected to participate fully in all scheduled sessions.

About the Project

The Safe Space Fellowship is a fictional in-world artifact from SAFE SPACE, a horror-dark comedy feature screenplay by Trevor Zhou.

When a legendary horror producer invites a group of emerging writers of color to his vineyard retreat, they think they’ve finally gotten their big break. But as they’re killed off one by one, they discover his celebrated career was built on sacrifice. Theirs.

This site is not a real fellowship, retreat, contest, employment opportunity, or development program.
